RESEARCH

Understanding galaxies across cosmic time.

My work combines observations and simulations to study the formation and evolution of galaxies, especially in the early Universe.

01

Observations

Using JWST, Hubble, ALMA and the VLT to uncover the first billion years of cosmic history.

02

Synthetic observations

Developing Synthesizer to create realistic virtual observations from simulations.

03

Simulations

Co-leading FLARES to explore the first galaxies and the early Universe.

TEACHING & ADMINISTRATION

Teaching, leadership
and academic community.

I supervise PhD students and teach across astronomy, physics and data science. I am committed to helping students develop independent research skills, curiosity and confidence.

As Head of Physics and Astronomy, I provide strategic and operational leadership for a department of more than 150 staff and PhD students, supporting the delivery of research and teaching at scale.

PUBLIC ENGAGEMENT

Science belongs to everyone.

Public engagement is central to my work: creating experiences that connect astronomy and discovery with audiences of all ages.

From community events and immersive experiences to talks and exhibitions, I work with partners across science, culture and education to make research more accessible.

PUBLIC PROGRAMMES

Talks and events

More than 100 public talks in the UK and internationally, from science festivals and public lectures to schools and community events.

EXPERIENCES & CONSULTANCY

Exhibitions, XR and Syzygy Labs

Lead exhibitor at the Royal Society Summer Science Exhibition and a scientific consultant for museums and media projects. Through Syzygy Labs, I build engaging exhibits and offer scientific consultancy for creative, cultural and commercial partners.

COMMUNITY PARTNERSHIPS

Curiosity Sussex and Herstmonceux

Founder and chair of Curiosity Sussex, delivering free science activities across Sussex. I am also Astronomer in Residence at the Observatory Science Centre and trustee of Science Projects.
